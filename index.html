Arjuna said: O Janardana, as told by you if ஸுக்தி is considered superior to action, then O Kesava why do you urge me to engage in this terrible action of war.

Arjuna's question in this verse has arisen on the advice given in கூரேண ப்யவரம் கர்ம—II-49. Arjuna's doubt amounts to this: if one can make one's ஸுக்தி equable like that of a ஸ்திதப்ரஜ்ஞ it is quite sufficient one can remain quite like Sankya Sanyasi without performing actions. This is just what Arjuna was prepared to do. Then there is no necessity to wage war & perform actions.
